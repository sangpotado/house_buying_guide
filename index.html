3. The Step-by-Step Process: CA vs Colorado

Follow the chronological steps of a real estate transaction and see how state laws shape each phase.

🌴

California

Escrow State • Prop 13 Taxes • Dry Funding

Step 1
Offer Accepted & Escrow Opens
You sign the standard C.A.R. RPA (Residential Purchase Agreement). Earnest money (usually 1-3%) is deposited into an independent Escrow Company, which acts as a neutral third party to manage funds and docs.
Step 2
Review Disclosures & Taxes
The seller provides a TDS (Transfer Disclosure Statement) listing known defects and an NHD (Natural Hazard Disclosure) for fire/earthquake zones.
Tax Law (Prop 13): Property taxes are calculated on your purchase price and capped from increasing more than 2% per year.
Step 3
Inspections & Contingency Removal
You have 17 days to do inspections (WDO/Termite is standard) and 21 days for loan approval. You must actively sign a "Contingency Removal" form to proceed. Note: In highly competitive CA markets, buyers often waive these contingencies upfront to win the bid.
Step 4
Closing (Dry Funding)
CA uses "Dry Funding." You will sign loan documents with a notary 1-3 days before closing. The lender reviews them and sends funds later. You only get the keys once the deed is physically recorded at the county clerk's office.
🏔️

Colorado

Title State • Market Taxes • Wet Funding

Step 1
Offer Accepted & Title Opens
You use the state-mandated CBS1 contract. Instead of escrow, a Title Company handles both the title work and holds the earnest money.
Step 2
Review Disclosures & Taxes
Seller provides disclosures, specifically a Source of Water Addendum (crucial in CO) and a Methamphetamine disclosure.
Tax Law (Biennial Reassessment): CO reassesses property values every two years based on market rates (Assessed Value × Local Mill Levy). If the market booms, taxes can jump significantly.
Step 3
Inspections & Strict Deadlines
CO contracts have "drop-dead" deadlines for inspection objections and HOA document reviews. If you don't object by the exact date, your earnest money goes "hard" (becomes non-refundable) and you accept the home as-is.
Step 4
Closing (Wet / Table Funding)
CO uses "Table Funding." Everyone meets at the Title Company. You sign documents, the lender wires the funds immediately while you sit there, and you walk out with the keys that exact same day.

Pro tip: Inspection Objection & Credits

Finding a major issue doesn’t have to kill the deal. Submit a written Inspection Objection. Instead of asking the seller to fix it (they will likely choose the cheapest possible contractor just to close the deal), ask for a Seller Credit toward your closing costs. This allows you to hire a professional you trust to do the repairs properly after you move in.
